Frequently Asked Questions

Get quick answers to common personal finance questions.

Personal finance is the management of your money, including budgeting, saving, investing, insurance, and retirement planning. It encompasses all financial decisions and activities of an individual or household.

A common rule of thumb is the 50/30/20 rule: allocate 50% of income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings and debt repayment. However, if you can save more—especially for retirement or emergency funds—that's even better.

An emergency fund is a cash reserve specifically set aside for unplanned expenses or financial emergencies, such as medical bills, car repairs, or job loss. Most experts recommend saving 3 to 6 months' worth of living expenses.

Start by paying off high-interest debt and building an emergency fund. Then, open a brokerage or retirement account, begin with low-cost index funds or ETFs, and invest consistently over time. The key is to start early and stay invested.
